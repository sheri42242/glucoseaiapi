What is Diabetes

Diabetes is a serious, chronic condition that occurs when the body cannot produce enough insulin or cannot effectively use the insulin it does produce. Insulin is an essential hormone produced in the pancreas that helps your body turn food into energy and controls your blood sugar levels.
Diabetes has two types:

Type 1: Genetic Disorder where immune system kills the Insulin. It cannot be prevented.
Type 2: Pancreas Stops or decreases insulin production. It can be prevented.

An insulin deficit, if left unchecked over the long term, can cause damage to many of the body’s organs, leading to life-threatening health complications such as cardiovascular diseases (CVD), nerve damage (neuropathy), kidney damage (nephropathy), lower-limb amputation, and eye disease (mainly affecting the retina) resulting in visual loss and even blindness.

Quick Facts about Diabetes

Type 2 diabetes accounts for the vast majority (over 90%) of diabetes worldwide

An estimated 537 million adults aged 20–79 years are currently living with diabetes. This represents 10.5% of the world’s population in this age group.

Almost 90% of people with undiagnosed diabetes live in low- and middleincome countries

In Africa, South-East Asia and the Western Pacific more than half of people with diabetes are undiagnosed



How it Works

Through our AI-powered diabetes prediction tool, we utilize advanced algorithms to analyze your input data, including factors such as age, BMI, and family history, to predict your HbA1c level. HbA1c is a crucial diagnostic method used to determine a person's average blood sugar level over the past 2-3 months, providing valuable insight into their risk for developing diabetes. By accurately predicting your HbA1c level, our tool can help identify individuals who may be at risk for diabetes and allow them to take proactive steps towards managing their health.
